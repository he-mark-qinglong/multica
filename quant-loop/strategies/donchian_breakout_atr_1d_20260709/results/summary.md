All three symbols: positive return, positive Sharpe, profit factor > 1, max
drawdown < 0.5%. The strategy is a **directional hedge for vpvr_reversion**:
when crypto trends down hard (2024-mid → 2026-mid in this sample), the
buy-hold benchmark bleeds 6-55%, but the trend strategy still grinds out
flat-to-positive with sub-1% drawdowns. The trade-off is capacity — 1%
notional per signal caps the absolute return, by design.

## Why this works in this window

The 1m klines we use start in April 2024 — peak crypto. From there BTC/ETH/SOL
all enter a sustained downtrend (with intermittent bear-market rallies). Trend
filters (ADX>20, ATR above 70% of its 100d mean) suppress most ranging/chop
entries, so the strategy is short-biased for most of the window. Shorts are
where the 0.30-0.50 win-rates come from on ETH/SOL — the trend persisted long
enough for trailing stops to keep the winners running.

## Files

- `trades_<SYMBOL>.csv` — every closed trade with reason + pnl
- `summary.json` — machine-readable metrics
- `data/manifest.parquet.sha256` — SHA256 of the 3 source 1m parquets
- `data/fapi_<SYMBOL>__1d.parquet` — 1d cache (re-derivable from 1m)

## Caveats

- This is iteration #1, single-pass, no walk-forward CV, no parameter search.
- The 1% per-signal cap makes the absolute returns small; whether to scale
  up is the next decision (risk-managed at the portfolio level in EPIC-D).
- No funding-rate modelling — for perpetuals this would overstate short
  performance slightly. Out of scope for the bootstrap.
- The "atr_trailing" anchor is the entry price, not a ratcheting low/high.
  A ratchet variant is on the EPIC-D roadmap.
